Glacê - Easter Social Campaign

Art direction | Mar-Apr.2017

Glacê Gourmet is a small home confectionery, who offered homemade Chocolate Eggs and a Box of Sweets on last Easter.All the selling of the products were by social network messaging, so I helped to create a campaign to exhibit the products.

Exactly one month before the Easter date, we publish a poster remembering that Easter was coming. The aim of this post was to give a sense of urgency, stimulate the public to think about what they would buy as a gift for their family and friends and invite them to talk to Glacê to see the great options of Chocolate Eggs and the Box of Sweets.

The second post os focused on the sweets and the possibility of personalization. Glacê made it possible to the client choosing all the flavors that would fit into the Box of Sweets. Therefore the post was showing the picture of the box, and the flavors available.

A cover to the Box of Sweets was made, to highlit this option of the product.

A consistency of the communication was maintained in the box of the Chocolate Egg, wich it took a sticker with the same visual appeal and wishing Happy Easter.

ContaAzul - New website

UX, UI | February 2017

ContaAzul is one of the largest startups in Brazil that develops a financial system for small companies and accountants. Your website is one of the most important channels for converting visitors to leads, which increases the importance of this project, that aimed to improve the conversion rate through a better user experience.

It was known that the site had some broken points in the user's journey, so the first step was to map all the available content in an inventory, to find and fix the unlinked pages, making a new flow. When the inventory was ready, we realized that the site had 8 different navigation menu and 8 different footers, and this was making the user confused, it was difficult to find where to go, or how to get back, to find the content that was seen before.

So, in the second stage, a rearrangement of the content, a new Architectural Information of the site was made. ContaAzul has two distinct audiences, the entrepreneurs, and the accountants, so that was necessary clearly identify which content was being shown at every time. The best hypothesis found was fixing a content category filter - 'entrepreneur', 'accountants' and 'about the company' - before the main navigation, making easier for the user realize how to find their space, and more important than that, where login in the system.

Thinking of a better user experience, the Jobs To Be Done methodology was applied, to make the content organization feel more natural, having the problems that ContaAzul helps users solve in mind. To find the Jobs To Be Done that has fit with the ContaAzul public, we did a survey with the current clients at that time, asking what were the problems that were solved with the help of ContaAzul's features. As result of this survey, we find some common problems to all users, that could be applied as Jobs to Be Done. We filtered the most relevant in the number of choices, or in the strategy of business, and we applied a new research, an online Card Sorting method, to find and validate how to organize the features in those Jobs To Be Done.

With the new content hierarchy and the final Jobs to Be Done, the next step consisted in re-layout the main page of the site, and for that we use storytelling.

In the header, we wanted to show a confident entrepreneur, showing how does it feel using ContaAzul, how his business life is calmer, and his company is more effective. ContaAzul has a guideline based on illustrations, so we use a character, making him look to the main email input and call to action button, which is a nice conversion practice.

After showing the ContaAzul successful client, we present how ContaAzul helped him to get there, showing the features related with their Job's To Be Done. We focus on showing the screen of the system that represents better the problem solving of which job.

Beyond the helpful features, ContaAzul has the purpose of helping the entrepreneurs' success, and they do this through an Online University with a free educational material. So a section dedicated to showing the main categories of the University, such as webinars, courses, and guides.

Some current clients, that have being using ContaAzul, confirm their efficiency and tell how it improve their business. As social proof, this section centralized some small videos of clients reporting their experience using ContaAzul. The ideia was display the deppoiments as a carrossel and show the videos only when the user decide to, clicking on the play button.

To end the story of the site, we invite the user to join the currently numerous entrepreneurs that have been improving their business with ContaAzul. This Call to Action intend to have a great conversion rate, and we use two growth hacking good practices as the urgency sentence "start now" and the idea of no commitment with the sentence "Your card number is not asked".

I had the opportunity to be the main designer conducting this project and define some points of direction but was really amazing working with all areas of the company, researching clients, to get rich insights and develop best hypothesis. Unfortunately, I couldn't follow the validation and the develop implementation because I had to left the company to move to another country with my husband. But it is still ongoing with ContaAzul team.

Personal Project - Wedding

Visual Identity | Jan.2017

This project has a special meaning because it was the communication of my wedding. Our wedding party was a farewell too because we were moving to another country, so we used this context plus our love for traveling in the concept of the wedding visual identity. ContaAzul - LP Academy

UX, UI | November 2016

ContaAzul is a startup that develops a financial and accountant system. Their marketing strategy is hugely based on inbound marketing which provides educative materials, - like ebooks, guides, infographics, - about some subject for the users, but in their case, always related to entrepreneurship. The free materials were shared in emails or posts on the social networks, and through them, the user was directed to a download Landing Page.

A page with all materials had existed, but it was outdated regarding layout, which had the old visual style guide of the brand, and bad usability (it was not working on mobile devices). A technical structure issue was considered too, which the old page had been located in an old directory, and it had to be moved to new one, in Hubspot.

So the project briefing was re-layout the Landing Page of Materials, considering some points:

  • It had to follow the existent structure of pages built in Hubspot;
  • The assets of the materials couldn't be changes, it had to follow that dimension;
  • It had to be simple to update - it was all manually;
  • It had to be easy to find the categories of the materials;
  • It had to be ready in one week (the layout)- at the end, I have had 3 or 4 days to do it.

What I like about this project is the structure of the filter which makes all the information of the materials archive been available in the same time. You just need to interact with the filter to see the in-depth of materials.

Considering the limitations of the scope of the project and the tight deadline, I was satisfied to provide an objective and clear user experience and solve the project goals.
